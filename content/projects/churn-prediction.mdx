## Problem Statement

High customer churn directly impacts revenue and growth. Understanding which customers are at risk and why enables data-driven retention strategies. This project builds a predictive model coupled with explainability analysis to identify at-risk segments.

## Exploratory Data Analysis

The dataset consists of 50,000 customer records spanning 18 behavioral and demographic features:

- Contract length, monthly charges, tenure
- Service usage patterns (internet, phone, streaming)
- Customer demographics (age, family status)
- Support interaction counts, payment method

Initial churn rate: **26.5%** — a significant business problem.

### Model Training with XGBoost

```python
import xgboost as xgb
from sklearn.model_selection import train_test_split
from sklearn.metrics import roc_auc_score

X_train, X_test, y_train, y_test = train_test_split(
    df_encoded.drop('churn', axis=1),
    df_encoded['churn'],
    test_size=0.2,
    random_state=42,
)

model = xgb.XGBClassifier(
    n_estimators=200,
    max_depth=6,
    learning_rate=0.1,
    random_state=42,
)

model.fit(X_train, y_train, eval_set=[(X_test, y_test)], verbose=False)

y_pred_proba = model.predict_proba(X_test)[:, 1]
auc_score = roc_auc_score(y_test, y_pred_proba)
print(f"ROC-AUC Score: {auc_score:.4f}")
```

## Business Impact

The model identifies high-risk customers with 82% precision. Targeted retention campaigns reduce predicted churn by 18% and deliver 3.2× ROI.
